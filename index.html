About

I'm a computer science new grad who likes the unglamorous half of software: the parts that have to be correct under failure. Consensus algorithms, query planners, packet headers, index structures: the machinery that everything else trusts.

Most of what I learn, I learn by building it badly first and then making it fast. I run a self-hosted home lab (Docker, Plex, Pi-hole, monitoring, a Minecraft server) on real metal, which is where a lot of my systems instincts come from. Nothing teaches failover like your own services going down at 2am.

Coursework has hit compilers, databases, networking, and HCI. Outside of that I'm a long-time PC gamer and tinkerer, which is also where most of my side projects start.
